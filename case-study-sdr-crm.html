SDR CRM, RevOps Build

Built a fully functional CRM with mobile app, web dashboard, and AI call summaries, for $23/month instead of $750.

SDR teams paying $750+/month for Salesforce to do what a well-engineered Google Sheets backend can do for almost nothing. This is the production-grade alternative, built to ship, not to demo.

1. The Problem

SDR teams paying $750+/month for Salesforce to do what a well-engineered Google Sheets backend can do for almost nothing. The pain isn't the tool, it's that the founders running these teams know the tool is bloated for what they actually do, but the alternatives all need engineering they don't have.

2. What Was Built

  • Node.js + Express backend with Google Sheets as the database, atomic writes, schema-aware row updates, no race conditions.
  • Next.js web CRM, kanban pipeline (7 stages), lead list, CSV import, real-time polling every 10 seconds.
  • React Native mobile app, post-call voice memo → Deepgram transcription → Gemini AI summary → auto-logged to Sheet in under 10 seconds.
  • Full pipeline orchestrator, call happens, SDR records memo, transcript generated, AI summary produced, Sheet updated atomically. End to end, no manual handoffs.

3. Hard Problems Solved

  • Android suspends microphone during phone calls, redesigned the entire flow to a post-call voice memo model. SDR taps record, summarises in their own words, ships.
  • Phone numbers with leading + signs stripped by Google Sheets (treated as formulas), fixed by switching to RAW input on the Sheets API.
  • Gemini hallucinating summaries on empty transcripts, added an empty-transcript guard before the model call.
  • API key exposure, the AI key never touches the browser. Next.js Route Handler proxy injects the key server-side, request-by-request.
Screenshots

This isn't a demo. It's a production-grade RevOps tool built with a seller's understanding of what SDRs actually need on a call.

  • Full-stack JS engineering, Node, Next.js, React Native
  • API-first design, Sheets, Deepgram, Gemini, all orchestrated end to end
  • Mobile-first product instinct, built for the second after a call ends, not the dashboard view
  • RevOps cost discipline, $23/mo, not $750/mo, no functional compromise
What I'd do at your company

I'd build your internal RevOps tools the same way I built this, sales-instinct first, engineering second, vendor bills last. Most SaaS in your stack is paying tax on capability you could ship in two weeks with the right operator. I'd find the three line items where that's most true, and replace them with tools your team actually wants to use. Production-grade, not toy versions, but built without the SaaS markup.
